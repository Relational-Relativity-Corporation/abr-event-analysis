# Role Separation and Operator Application

**Metatron Dynamics, Inc.** V6. Reference data. Bounded over D. No claim beyond D.

Roles are not named into existence. They are the functions performed, and they reconstruct from the constraints that must hold for the workflow to function.

**Constraints.**

1. **No declaration, no evaluation** — there is nothing to evaluate before something is declared. More precisely: without a declared D and a declared M, there is no observable to trace quantities back to. Evaluation without this grounding is not constrained computation — it is computation about nothing in D.
2. **Function defines role, not the label** — a role exists when its function is performed, not when the word appears.
3. **Description is not instantiation** — a document about a function is not the function.
4. **Responsibility stays with what has agency** — it cannot migrate to operators, documents, frameworks, or models, none of which have any. In the current implementation domain, this is the human.
5. **Verification detects divergence; it does not approve** — approval is downstream.
6. **Generation and verification require distinct frames** — a frame cannot detect the errors in its own blind spot.
7. **Verification is collaborative** — distinct frames may include both human and AI participants. Verification is strengthened when independent observers compare the evolving relational state against the same declarations. Neither participant is presumed free of drift.
8. **Scale does not alter declared roles** — increasing computational scale, information volume, or processing speed changes how the declared roles are performed. It does not alter what the roles are or who holds them.
9. **Roles are declared independently of implementation** — changing software architecture, computational platform, processing agent, or execution environment does not redefine the declared roles.

**The roles, as they reconstruct.**

- **Origin** — declaration and responsibility: declares D, M, the relations and their provenance, and what is admissible. AI extends the range over which the declared operators may be applied; it does not assume Origin. The operators extend observation beyond unaided human perception where the declared observable permits; they do not transfer agency. Declaration, admissibility, and disposition of findings remain with Origin throughout the declared workflow. *Origin declares.*
- **Generator** — construction: proposes candidate structure within what Origin declared. *Generator proposes.*
- **Verifier** — divergence detection against declared criteria, from a frame distinct from the Generator. Verification may be performed collaboratively by human and AI observers operating from independent frames. Findings return to Origin for disposition. *Verifier observes.*

Origin is held by the human and is not assignable to a frame; the human cannot delegate out of it without vacating it.

**Application — the workflow.**

1. **Declare.** Origin declares D and M, the relations and their source, and checks admissibility: every declared relation must have a single admissible direction traceable to an observable through M; no relation fabricated; no boundary closed to supply continuation that was not declared. Where both directions of a relation are declared, Origin verifies independent observable provenance for each direction and applies the distinctness axiom — if both directions are declared, they are treated as distinct relations, not reverse observations of the same relation. If no declaration is admissible, stop — the operators are undefined, not approximate.

2. **Generate.** The kernel applied depends on the declared relational structure. At the Primary Region — where persistence is not yet confirmed and B is not active — apply Δ → Σ → E_primary over the declared spatial relations. When persistence is confirmed (ρ_P → 1) — apply A → B → R → E over the declared relations: Phase 1 applies A → B → R over spatial relations to produce the spatial relational field; Phase 2 applies A → B → R over persistence relations — connecting the prior complete kernel output to the current one across one declared relational step — to produce the relational-evolution field. Both phases use the same operator formulas. The declared relational step is the processor's own oscillation cycle, not an externally imposed increment. Apply any projection as a declared one — state what it preserves and what it discards.

3. **Verify.** One or more observers, operating from frames distinct from the Generator and from each other, detect divergence from the declared criteria and return located findings — not approval. Findings include: whether any declared relation lacks independent provenance for its declared direction; whether the declared edge-image is admissible (asymmetric); whether any failure mode is detected; whether the declared projection states what it preserves and discards.

4. **Revise.** Findings return to Origin; the constraints are refined; the pass repeats. Roles alternate across distinct frames between passes.

*The operators hold no agency; whatever the workflow commits to, a person committed it. Origin remains human because declaration and responsibility cannot be delegated. Generation and verification, however, are collaborative activities in which both human and AI may contribute from distinct frames. Because the declared observable includes the contributions of both participants, both remain subject to relational observation.*

*Bounded over D. No claim is made beyond D.*
